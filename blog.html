Balboa's first time on a boat: a new adventure!

Have you ever been on a boat? If so, then you know the feeling of excitement that comes with it. If not, then imagine the wind in your hair and the sun on your face as you cruise across the water. Now, imagine that feeling times a hundred – that's how Balboa felt when he first got on a boat!

This was a whole new experience for Balboa, and he loved every minute of it. He spent the entire time running around, exploring every inch of the boat. He even made some new friends along the way!
If you're looking for an adventure, then getting on a boat with your dog is the way to go. And if you're like Balboa, then you'll have the time of your life!
Balboa felt very excited when he first got on the boat. It was a beautiful day, and the sun was shining. The boat ride was going to be a great adventure. He could not wait to explore the lake. As the boat started moving, Balboa felt the wind in his fur and the sun on his face. He was surrounded by blue sky and green trees. It was a perfect day for a boat ride.
Balboa had a great time during his boat adventure. He loved being on the lake and excitedly ran around the boat. He had so much fun that he didn't want to leave when it was time to dock the boat.

Thanks for reading about Balboa's first time on a boat! I hope you enjoyed hearing about his adventure.

Balboa's Road Trip Adventures: A Dog's-Eye View

Balboa and I hit the highway early Friday morning. I turned up the music and rolled down the windows for Balboa. Anticipating the truck accelerating to highway speeds, Balboa promptly stuck his head out the rear driver's side window...He's got a need for speed. I watched him in the side mirror, his ears pinned back and gums flapping against the wind...pure happiness at that moment for the both of us.

We drove for hours, making a couple of stops along the way. The first was at a prairie field where Balboa chased a weird bird with a skinny, long neck. I know he was trying to figure out if it was "prey or play?". He'd be a great hunter if I let him off the long lead, but he sure does enjoy the chase... especially when we bring the bike.

The next stop was at Horseshoe Canyon. We took a short hike and I let Balboa off his leash to explore. He ran around like crazy, taking in all the new smells and sounds. I think he liked it there...I know I did.

After Horseshoe Canyon, we made our way to Drumheller - home of the Badlands and Dinosaur bones! We walked around for a while; Balboa peed on everything as usual. If you have a dog, you know they can always tell when they're someplace new, Balboa is no exception. He was enjoying himself; smelling, and peeing on new things are among his top 10 favorite activities. In dog world, Balboa is a real estate mogul. He’s got a spritz for every block in every town.

We drove west towards Sylvan Lake where Balboa enjoyed a refreshing dip... only up to the shoulders of course. He loves to frolic near and in the water, but never too deep. He thoroughly despises swimming. Sometimes I wonder if he's truly a dog.

After our time at the lake, we stopped for lunch on a restaurant patio. I ordered a steak and Balboa got his usual - also steak with some water on the side. We sat there, people-watching and enjoying each other's company. It was a perfect day.

We stayed overnight in a hotel and hit the road again early the next morning. We crossed a muddy trail and got stuck in the mud...I should've known better than to steer into that soft soil. Luckily, some off-roaders were driving by, and they towed us out. After thanking the off-roaders and proceeding with a little more caution, we spent some time walking through the woods near the trail before getting back in the truck and heading home.

Balboa slept almost the whole way back. He had a great time on our little road trip and I'm sure he's looking forward to our next adventure...wherever that may be. Thanks for joining us on our journey! Until next time...
